静岡大学にてアルバータ大学のJerome C. Foo先生の講演会が行われ、その後金研究室に訪問していただきました。


講演タイトル：
Towards Precision Mental Health: Genes and Environment
プレシジョン・メンタルヘルスの実現に向けて：遺伝子と環境


講師：Jerome C. Foo先生　（出版物：https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=kBV7068AAAAJ&hl=en）
Department of Genetic Epidemiology in Psychiatry, Central Institute of Mental Health, Medical Faculty Mannheim, University of Heidelberg, Mannheim, Germany
Institute for Psychopharmacology, Central Institute of Mental Health, Medical Faculty Mannheim, University of Heidelberg, Mannheim, Germany
College of Health Sciences, Department of Psychiatry, University of Alberta, Edmonton, Alberta, Canada
Neuroscience and Mental Health Institute, University of Alberta, Edmonton, Alberta, Canada


概要：
The rates of mental illness diagnoses have increased significantly in recent years, driven in part by greater awareness and reduced stigma, but also due to the impact of world events. How genetics and their interaction with the environment (G x E) influence the development of psychiatric disorders has been a subject of intensive study; it has been discovered that the risk for various disorders is driven by shared genetic factors. Together with recent technology-driven advances in our ability to identify and map the genes involved and explore how they interact with the environment to produce mental health outcomes, we need to move towards the translation of these insights into real-life contexts. An important component of this is having a better understanding of how heterogeneity and variation in G x E are involved.
This journey is not straightforward. As we move from discovery on the candidate and genome-wide scales to epigenetic mechanisms, to the identification of biomarkers and the implementation of pharmacogenomics, there are both scientific and systemic barriers which need to be overcome in order to successfully implement a ‘Precision Mental Health’ framework. Breaking down these barriers to tackle the significant societal burdens of mental illnesses requires a concerted effort to translate findings from research to education, healthcare and policy. To illustrate these topics, this talk will touch on historical and current topics in psychiatric genetics, neuropsychopharmacology and pharmacogenomics, presenting several ongoing studies aimed at tackling knowledge and implementation gaps.
